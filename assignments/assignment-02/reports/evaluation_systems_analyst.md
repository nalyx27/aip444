## 2. RESUME ADAPTATION

Given your background in IT Support, Computer Programming and Networking, you can reframe your experience to highlight transferable skills applicable to a Business/Systems Analyst role.

*   **Reorder "Skills" Section:** Move your "Hard Skills" and "Soft Skills" sections higher up in your resume, immediately after your contact information and summary (if you have one). This makes it easier for recruiters to quickly identify relevant skills.
*   **Expand on "Infotech Computers" Experience:** Even without specific "achievements," describe the *types* of tasks and responsibilities you handled. For example:
    *   "Provided technical support to clients, troubleshooting hardware and software issues."
    *   "Configured and maintained network infrastructure for small businesses."
    *   "Assisted in the implementation of new IT systems and solutions."
*   **Reframe IT Support Experience to Business Needs:** Emphasize how your IT support experience indirectly dealt with "business requirements." Example:
    *   Instead of: "Fixed computer problems," use "Identified and resolved IT issues impacting business operations, ensuring minimal downtime."
    *   Instead of: "Installed software," use "Deployed new software solutions, addressing specific business needs for enhanced efficiency and productivity."
*   **Highlight Problem-Solving Skills With Context:** Don't just list "Creative Problem Solving." Provide examples, even if they are from academic projects or personal projects. "Demonstrated creative problem-solving skills by developing [x] solution for [y] problem during [z] project in my diploma."
*   **Add a "Projects" Section (if possible):** Even small projects demonstrate initiative. Include brief descriptions highlighting your problem-solving abilities and technical skills. Relate these to a potential IT Business need that this role would deal with.
*   **Quantify When Possible:** Even in IT support, you might be able to quantify your impact: "Reduced average support ticket resolution time by 15% through proactive troubleshooting."
*   **Targeted Skills Section:** *Add* specific skills from the job description as keywords. Even if you only have basic knowledge, including them shows you've paid attention. Be prepared to discuss them in an interview. Include a new section that is for technologies from the *required* skills section from the posting, and include "Basic knowledge of X skill". This shows a willingness to learn.

## 4. INTERVIEW PREP

Prepare for questions designed to assess your analytical skills, problem-solving abilities, and potential for growth in a Business/Systems Analyst role.

*   **Behavioral Questions:**
    *   "Tell me about a time you had to solve a complex problem." (Use the STAR method - Situation, Task, Action, Result.)
    *   "Describe a situation where you had to learn a new skill quickly."
    *   "Give an example of how you have used communication skills to explain technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders."
    *   "Tell me about a time you had to work with a difficult client or customer. How did you handle the situation?"
*   **Technical Questions (Basic):**
    *   "What is a database?"
    *   "Explain the difference between front-end and back-end development."
    *   "What are the different types of programming paradigms?"
    *   "Do you know the difference between Waterfall and Agile project management methodologies?"
    *   "What are requirements gathering, documentation, and/or process mapping?"
*   **Role-Specific Questions:**
    *   "Why are you interested in transitioning into a Business/Systems Analyst role?"
    *   "What do you know about requirements gathering and analysis?"
    *   "How would you approach documenting a complex process?"
    *   "Give me an example of how you would solve a business requirement using IT."
*   **Company-Specific Questions:**
    *   "What do you know about CWB Group?"
    *   "Why do you want to work for CWB Group?"
*   **Skills to Brush Up On:**
    *   **Requirements Elicitation Techniques:** Research common methods like interviews, surveys, workshops, and document analysis.
    *   **Process Mapping:** Familiarize yourself with flowcharting and BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation).
    *   **Basic Project Management Concepts:** Understand the project lifecycle, key project roles, and common project management tools.
    *   **SQL Basics:** While not explicitly listed, SQL is fundamental for data-related tasks. Knowing the basics of SQL queries would be very helpful for a System/Business analyst.
*   **Potential Talking Points:**
    *   Your enthusiasm for learning and your adaptability.
    *   Your understanding of the importance of IT alignment with business goals.
    *   Your proactive approach to problem-solving.
    *   Your genuine interest in CWB Group's mission and values.
